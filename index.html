Story

A stonecutter worked hard every day in the mountains. His life was simple, and at first he was satisfied. One day, he saw a rich man living in a palace with beautiful furniture. The stonecutter thought, “That life is better than mine. I want to be rich.” Suddenly, his wish came true.

Soon, the rich man sat in his carriage, but the hot sun shone above him. “The sun is mightier than a rich man,” he thought. “I want to be the sun.” So he became the sun. Then a dark cloud covered him. “The cloud is mightier than the sun,” he said. “I want to be a cloud.” So he became a cloud.

After that, the wind pushed the cloud across the sky. “The wind is mightier than the cloud. I want to be the wind.” So he became the wind. But when he blew against a great mountain, the mountain did not move. “The mountain is mightier than the wind,” he thought. “I want to be a mountain.”

At last, he became a mountain. He felt strong and powerful. But then he felt someone cutting into him with a sharp tool. It was a stonecutter! The mountain thought, “The stonecutter is mightier than the mountain.” Then he understood. He had compared himself again and again, but he had never learned to be happy. He wished to be himself again, and he became a stonecutter.

Comprehension

1. At the beginning of the story, what job did the man have?
2. Why did he want to become the sun?
3. Which thing stopped the wind?
4. What did the man finally learn?
